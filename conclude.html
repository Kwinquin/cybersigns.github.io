The world of cyberpunk, though filled with fantastical gadgets and futuristic jargon, still closely resembles the path of where our current society is heading today. It's a reality where every aspect of capitalism is corrupt and shown at its worst. It not only manifests in the characters and the plot, but the scenery too, with invasive marketing tactics that show our increasing dependence on these big corpos and their inescapable grip on us. The bright neon lights that attract consumers like a moth to a flame with the false hope of living THE ideal life. The big digital billboards that try to influence what we listen to or buy when we're simply trying to walk down the street. These signs and advertisements are at their most intertwined with our day-to-day lives now more than ever and it begs the question: how do we avoid handing more and more power to the corporate system to keep our own individuality?

EXTRACTING [FINAL]...

Signs appear both in our everyday lives and in the lives of those who inhabit desolate cyberpunk environments. In the world of fiction, corporate powerhouses and greedy CEOs take advantage of their grip on the people, as seen with the bright colored signs and advertisements that take the individual out of the picture in favor of a product, a homogenization, and an ideal of what to consume and what to be like. In a sense, our very own world today inches closer and closer to the scary depictions of corporate control seen in media, with our world's advancing technology allowing for more advanced methods of marketing. Without taking a step back to reflect on the importance of ideas like democracy, individuality, and an effort for the good of the people rather than personal gain, we could very well stray into this cyberpunk dystopia. With technology today not just about implementing the gigantic billboards or screens imagined in cyberpunk media in the past, but also on the very devices we use via methods like pop-ups or videos to access certain sites. How do we avoid handing more and more power to the corporate system at our own loss?
